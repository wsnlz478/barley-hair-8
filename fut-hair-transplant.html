Grasping the FUT Approach

Follicular Unit Transplantation, commonly known as the strip technique, remains one of the most dependable and time-proven methods for permanent hair restoration. During the procedure, the surgeon removes a narrow band of tissue from the back of your scalp — the area genetically resistant to male pattern baldness. This tissue is then carefully separated into individual follicular units using high-powered microscopes, and each graft is precisely positioned into regions showing thinning or complete baldness.

Although FUE has gained significant popularity recently, FUT remains the gold standard for patients who need the maximum number of grafts in a single session. At Barley Hospital, we have advanced the traditional strip method by integrating modern trichophytic closure techniques. This refined approach enables hair to grow directly through the scar line, making it nearly invisible compared to older FUT methods still used at many clinics worldwide.

The FUT Journey: Step-by-Step Breakdown

What to Expect on Your Procedure Day

Your FUT journey begins with the surgeon applying local anaesthesia to the donor area at the back of your head, guaranteeing complete comfort. Once full numbness is achieved, a thin strip of tissue — typically 1 to 1.5 centimetres wide and extending up to 30 centimetres long — is carefully removed. The incision is then closed using our trichophytic suturing technique, an advanced method that layers the wound edges so hair follicles grow directly through the scar, making it nearly invisible.

After extraction, the tissue strip is passed to our specialised technical team. Using high-definition stereo microscopes, they meticulously separate the strip into individual follicular units, each containing one to four hairs. Meanwhile, your surgeon creates tiny recipient sites in the areas needing coverage. Every graft is positioned at the exact angle, depth, and direction to match your natural growth pattern. The entire procedure typically takes four to eight hours, depending on the total number of grafts needed.

Recovery Period and Scar Control

Your Post-Procedure Healing Journey

The healing period after FUT is slightly longer than FUE due to the linear incision along the back of the scalp. Most patients experience mild discomfort during the first two to three days, which is easily managed with prescribed medications. You can return to desk work within 3-5 days, though intense physical activity and heavy lifting should be avoided for at least 2 weeks to protect the healing incision.

Stitches or staples are removed 10-14 days after your procedure. The linear mark will gradually fade and soften over the following months. Thanks to our trichophytic closure technique, hair grows naturally through the scar line, making it barely noticeable as long as you keep at least 1-2 cm of hair length at the back of your head. Most patients find the scar is easily concealed by their existing hair and doesn't impact their daily confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions About FUT

Answers to the questions we hear most often from patients

How does the strip method produce more grafts than FUE?
FUT removes a continuous strip of tissue containing thousands of follicular units in one motion, which is then dissected under microscopes into individual grafts. Because the tissue is removed as a single piece, the surgeon can access the densest concentration of follicles without the time-consuming individual extraction required by FUE. This allows 3,000 to 5,000+ grafts in a single session, compared to the typical 2,500-3,500 graft ceiling with FUE. The efficiency of strip harvesting also means follicles spend less time outside the body, improving overall survival rates.
What is scalp laxity and why does it matter for FUT?
Scalp laxity refers to how loosely or tightly your scalp skin stretches. Patients with good laxity allow the surgeon to remove a wider strip without creating excessive tension when closing the wound. Low tension means a thinner, less visible scar and more comfortable healing. During your consultation, our surgeons will assess your scalp elasticity to determine the optimal strip width. If your scalp is particularly tight, we may recommend FUE instead, as the outcome could be compromised with FUT.
Can the FUT scar be repaired if a previous procedure left a bad one?
Yes, scar revision is one of the specialized services we offer. If a previous FUT procedure resulted in a widened or poorly closed scar, our surgeons can excise the old scar tissue and re-close the wound using modern trichophytic techniques. This allows hair to grow through the revised scar line, making it far less noticeable. Many patients who had disappointing FUT results elsewhere have come to Barley specifically for scar revision combined with additional graft placement.
How does FUT graft survival compare to FUE?
FUT grafts often have slightly higher survival rates than FUE because the follicles are removed as part of a protected tissue strip and spend less time exposed to air before being implanted. The microscope-assisted dissection process also allows technicians to carefully separate grafts without the risk of transection that can occur with punch-based extraction. At Barley, our FUT graft survival rates consistently reach 95-97%, which is comparable to or exceeds most FUE outcomes.
What happens if I need more grafts in the future after FUT?
If you need additional coverage after a FUT procedure, there are several options. A second FUT session may be possible if you have adequate remaining donor tissue and scalp laxity. Alternatively, FUE can be used to harvest additional grafts from the area above or below the original strip scar. Some patients benefit from a combination approach where FUE supplements the original FUT work. Our surgeons will evaluate your donor reserves and recommend the most appropriate strategy during your follow-up consultations.
Will the FUT scar stretch or widen over time?
With modern trichophytic closure and tension-reducing suturing techniques, significant scar widening is uncommon. However, individual healing varies based on genetics, skin type, and how well you follow post-operative care instructions. At Barley, we use a multi-layer closure approach that distributes tension across deeper tissue layers, minimizing the stress on the surface wound. Most patients find the scar remains thin and stable over time, especially when kept covered by hair at least 1-2 cm long.
Is FUT suitable for women with hair loss?
FUT can be suitable for women experiencing significant hair loss, particularly those with diffuse thinning patterns and adequate donor supply. However, female hair loss patterns differ from male patterns, and the decision between FUT and FUE depends on the specific case. Women who prefer to keep longer hair at the back of their scalp can easily conceal the FUT scar. During consultation, our surgeons will evaluate your hair loss pattern, donor density, and aesthetic goals to recommend the most appropriate technique.
How long does the FUT surgery actually take?
A typical FUT procedure takes between 4 and 6 hours, depending on the number of grafts required. The strip removal and closure take approximately 45-60 minutes, while the microscope dissection and graft implantation account for the remaining time. Larger sessions requiring 4,000+ grafts may extend to 7-8 hours. The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, and patients remain comfortable throughout. Many bring headphones, read, or even nap during the longer phases of the surgery.
What are the risks specific to FUT that don't apply to FUE?
FUT carries some unique considerations: the linear scar (though minimized with trichophytic closure), a slightly longer recovery period, and the possibility of temporary numbness or tingling in the donor area due to nerve disruption. There is also a small risk of wound dehiscence (separation) if the sutures are placed under excessive tension. However, these risks are significantly reduced with experienced surgeons and proper patient selection. At Barley, our complication rate for FUT is extremely low, and we thoroughly discuss all potential risks during your pre-operative consultation.
Can FUT be used for eyebrow or beard restoration?
While FUT is primarily used for scalp restoration, the technique can theoretically be adapted for eyebrow or beard work. However, FUE is generally preferred for facial hair restoration because it allows for more precise selection of individual follicles and avoids the linear scar. For scalp procedures requiring maximum graft volume, FUT remains the superior choice. If you need both scalp and facial restoration, we may recommend a combined approach using FUT for the scalp and FUE for the facial areas.
How does the cost of FUT compare to other countries?
FUT in China at Barley Hospital typically costs 50-70% less than equivalent procedures in the United States, United Kingdom, or Western Europe. For example, a 4,000-graft FUT session that might cost $12,000-$18,000 in the US can be performed at Barley for $3,500-$6,000, including all pre-operative assessments, the procedure itself, and post-operative care. Even after accounting for travel and accommodation expenses, international patients save thousands of dollars while receiving care from highly experienced surgeons using advanced techniques.
What type of anesthesia is used during FUT?
FUT is performed under local anesthesia, which numbs the donor and recipient areas completely. You remain awake throughout the procedure but feel no pain. Some patients request mild oral sedation to help them relax, which can be arranged. The initial anesthetic injections may cause a brief pinching sensation, but this subsides quickly. Once the area is fully numb, the procedure is entirely comfortable. After surgery, the anesthesia wears off gradually over 2-3 hours, and any discomfort is managed with prescribed pain medication.
Will FUT affect my ability to wear hats or helmets?
Once the donor area has fully healed (typically 2-3 weeks), you can wear hats, caps, and helmets without any issues. The scar is located at the back of the scalp and is well-protected by surrounding tissue. During the initial healing period, we recommend avoiding tight headwear that might put pressure on the wound. After complete healing, there are no permanent restrictions. Many of our patients work in construction, cycling, or motorcycle riding and wear helmets regularly without any problems related to their FUT scar.
How do I know if I have enough donor hair for FUT?
During your consultation, our surgeons will perform a thorough evaluation of your donor area, measuring both the density (follicles per square centimeter) and the total available surface area. They will also assess your scalp laxity and hair characteristics. Generally, patients with good density in the occipital region (back of the head) and adequate scalp flexibility are excellent candidates for FUT. If your donor supply is limited, we will discuss alternative options including FUE or body hair transplantation.
