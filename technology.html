FUE Method

FUE Hair Transplant

Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE) stands as the gold standard in modern hair restoration. Single follicular units are individually removed from the donor area using specialized micro-punches, then inserted with our microneedle pen to yield authentic-looking results with virtually no detectable scarring.

Gentle surgical approach
No strip scar left behind
Quick return to normal life
Genuine-looking regrowth

FUT Method

FUT Hair Transplant

Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT) entails extracting a narrow band of donor skin, which is then meticulously divided into separate follicular units. Although FUE remains our go-to technique, FUT remains a viable alternative for individuals who need the maximum graft count achievable in a single session.

Maximum graft yield
Cost-effective for larger zones

What to Expect From Start to Finish

A thorough nine-phase roadmap toward restoring your natural head of hair

01

Opening Consultation & Scalp Assessment

Your physician inspects your scalp, determines your degree of hair loss, and evaluates the follicle concentration in your donor area to confirm whether transplantation is the right path for you.

02

Your physician draws a bespoke hairline that complements your facial proportions, age group, and personal wishes. We chart every implantation zone and compute the exact graft count needed.

Standard blood work and coagulation tests are performed to eliminate any underlying health risks. Your wellbeing is always our foremost concern.

Targeted Numbing Injection

The donor and recipient zones are precisely anesthetized. You stay fully conscious and at ease, feeling zero pain throughout the entire procedure.

05

Donor Follicle Harvesting

Single follicular units are delicately removed from the donor zone at the rear of your head using refined micro-punches that leave only the tiniest, nearly undetectable traces.

06

Graft Preparation & Sorting

Our lab specialists thoroughly cleanse, organize, and preserve the collected follicles in a chilled nutrient bath to sustain peak viability right up until insertion.

07

Exact Follicle Insertion

Viable grafts are methodically placed one by one through our exclusive microneedle pen. The full 360° swivel ensures every graft follows the same trajectory as the surrounding natural hair.

Immediate Aftercare

Your scalp is softly cleaned and wrapped in a thin protective bandage. We make sure you feel at ease and walk you through detailed cleansing steps before you leave.

09

Healing & Continuous Follow-Up

You receive comprehensive written directions covering washing routines, everyday activities, and what to expect as you heal. We schedule check-in visits to monitor your advancement at every milestone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common inquiries about our microneedle hair restoration approach

What is the difference between FUE and FUT hair transplant?
FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) removes individual hair follicles one by one from the donor area using tiny punches, leaving no linear scar. FUT (Follicular Unit Transplantation) removes a strip of skin from the donor area, which is then dissected into individual follicles, leaving a linear scar. At Barley, we primarily use advanced microneedle FUE because it's less invasive, has faster recovery, and produces virtually invisible scarring. FUT is reserved for special cases requiring maximum grafts in one session.
How does microneedle technology differ from traditional FUE?
Traditional FUE uses forceps to place grafts into pre-made incisions — a two-step process. Our microneedle implant pen creates the incision and places the graft in one fluid motion using a 0.6-1.0mm needle. This reduces graft handling time by 60%, minimizes trauma to the follicle, and allows for tighter packing. The pen's 360-degree rotation ensures each graft is placed at the exact angle of your natural hair growth.
What is the 360-degree rotation feature and why does it matter?
Hair doesn't grow straight up — each follicle emerges at a unique angle and direction. Our implant pen's barrel rotates a full 360 degrees, allowing the surgeon to orient each graft to match your natural growth pattern precisely. Without this capability, hair can grow in the wrong direction, creating an unnatural "pluggy" appearance. This rotation is exclusive to Barley's patented technology and is critical for achieving undetectable results.
Why is the 0.6mm needle size significant?
At 0.6mm, our finest needle is about three times narrower than traditional transplant instruments. Smaller incisions mean less tissue trauma, faster healing, less bleeding and swelling, and the ability to place grafts closer together for higher density. Think of the difference between a needle prick and a paper cut — the smaller wound closes faster and leaves virtually no trace. This is why patients can wash their hair just 24 hours after surgery.
How does Barley's technology compare to DHI (Direct Hair Implantation)?
DHI is a general category for implant pen methods. The most well-known DHI tool is the Choi Implanter Pen. While both systems load grafts into a pen for direct insertion, Barley's technology offers two key advantages: our exclusive 360-degree rotation mechanism (not available in Choi pens) and a finer needle starting at 0.6mm versus Choi's minimum of about 0.7mm. These differences translate to better angle precision and faster recovery.
What's the difference between microneedle and Sapphire FUE?
Sapphire FUE uses sapphire crystal blades to create recipient sites, then grafts are placed with forceps — a two-step process. Our microneedle system performs incision and placement together in one motion via the implant pen. This combined action reduces graft handling time by over 60%, lowers the risk of mechanical damage during transfer, and enables tighter packing since the surgeon feels tissue resistance in real time.
What are Barley's 14 patents and what do they cover?
Our 14 patents cover the entire transplant workflow: the core implant pen design with 360-degree rotation, depth control systems for consistent placement, graft protection technology to prevent damage during handling, precision extraction tools, specialized beard harvesting devices, temperature-controlled storage systems for optimal viability, precision tweezers for gentle manipulation, and density optimization algorithms. These patents represent nearly 20 years of continuous innovation.
Can the microneedle pen be used for eyebrow and beard transplants?
Yes, the microneedle technology is excellent for eyebrow and beard restoration. Eyebrow hair grows at very specific angles that differ from scalp hair. The 360-degree rotation allows us to reproduce the natural feathered sweep of each eyebrow hair with precision that forceps methods cannot match. For beard transplants, we use specialized tools designed for coarser facial hair follicles. We even use finer needle configurations specifically tuned for the delicate eyebrow area.
How are grafts harvested during FUE?
Our surgical team uses motorized extraction punches ranging from 0.8mm to 1.0mm in diameter. Each punch carefully scores around a single follicular unit in the donor area at the back of your head. The follicle is then gently removed and immediately placed in a chilled preservation bath. Our harvesting and placement teams work in parallel to minimize the time each graft spends outside your body, maximizing survival rates.
When would you recommend FUT over FUE?
Our primary technique is microneedle FUE. FUT (the strip method) is reserved for specific situations: patients who need an exceptionally high graft count in a single session and have limited donor density, or cases where the patient's hair characteristics make strip harvesting more practical. FUT does leave a linear scar, so we discuss this trade-off thoroughly during your consultation. For most patients, FUE is the superior choice.
What is a no-shave hair transplant?
In a standard procedure, the donor area is shaved short for visibility. With our no-shave option, the surgical team extracts individual follicles from among your existing longer hair, and the recipient area is never shaved at all. This means nobody will know you've had a procedure. The trade-off is that the session takes longer since the team must navigate around your current hair. Results are identical to the conventional approach.
How are grafts stored during the procedure?
Every harvested follicle is placed in a specialized nutrient-enriched preservation solution maintained at 4-8 degrees Celsius (39-46 degrees Fahrenheit). This cold environment significantly slows cellular activity, extending the viability window for each graft. Our patented storage system, covered under our intellectual property, is one key reason our survival rates consistently exceed 95% even during long procedures.
Does Barley use AI or robotics during surgery?
We use AI-powered software for pre-operative hairline planning. The system analyzes your facial proportions, age, ethnic characteristics, and personal preferences to recommend the most natural hairline position and design. However, the actual extraction and placement are performed entirely by experienced surgeons. We believe surgical skill and human judgment remain essential for delivering truly natural results.
How many grafts can be transplanted in one session?
A typical session covers 2,000-3,500 grafts and takes 6-8 hours. For patients needing more extensive coverage, we can transplant up to 4,000+ grafts by spreading the work across two consecutive days. The limiting factor isn't the technology but rather your available donor supply and how long you can sit comfortably under local anesthesia. Your surgeon will recommend the optimal graft count during your consultation.
Why does Barley's technology achieve higher density than conventional methods?
Two factors drive the density advantage. First, the ultra-fine 0.6mm needle means each graft takes up less space, allowing closer spacing between adjacent follicles without compromising blood supply. Second, because we skip the pre-made incision step, we can adjust spacing dynamically during placement, fine-tuning distribution for maximum visual impact. The result is 20-30% higher density using the same number of grafts.
