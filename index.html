International Patient Questions Answered

Essential information for your hair transplant journey to China

Why should I choose China over Turkey or Mexico for my hair transplant?
China offers a unique combination of cutting-edge technology, experienced surgeons, and significant cost savings. While Turkey has become popular for medical tourism, China's hair transplant clinics like Barley have been pioneering microneedle technology since 2006 with 14 proprietary patents. You'll save 50-70% compared to US/UK prices while receiving care that meets or exceeds international standards. Plus, China's rich culture and modern cities make it an fascinating destination for combining your procedure with travel.
How do I know if I'm a good candidate for hair transplant?
Ideal candidates have stable hair loss, sufficient donor hair density at the back of the scalp, and realistic expectations. During your free video consultation, our surgeons will assess your hair loss pattern, donor area quality, and discuss whether transplant or alternative treatments would work best for you. We treat both men and women with various types of hair loss, and we'll be honest if another approach would better serve your goals.
What exactly is included in the quoted price?
Our transparent pricing covers the complete procedure: initial consultation, scalp analysis, surgery with local anesthesia, all medications for recovery, post-operative care products, and follow-up appointments for one full year. We also include complimentary airport pickup and hotel recommendations. The only additional costs are your flights, meals, and personal expenses. No hidden fees, no surprises on procedure day.
How long do I need to stay in China for the procedure?
Plan for 5-7 days total. Day 1: consultation and pre-op tests. Day 2: the surgery itself (4-8 hours). Days 3-5: initial recovery and follow-up check. Most patients fly home on day 5 or 6. If you want to sightsee, you can extend your stay — light tourism is fine after the first week once your surgeon clears you.
Will I experience pain during or after the procedure?
During the procedure, you'll receive local anesthesia so you feel no pain — only slight pressure. Many patients watch movies, listen to music, or even nap during the 4-8 hour surgery. Afterward, most report mild tenderness or tightness for 2-3 days, easily managed with over-the-counter pain relievers. Our microneedle technique causes minimal tissue trauma, so recovery is significantly more comfortable than traditional methods.
When will I see the final results?
Transplanted hair sheds within 2-4 weeks (this is normal), then new growth begins around month 3-4. You'll see significant improvement by month 6, with final results visible at 9-12 months as the hair thickens and matures. The waiting requires patience, but the results are permanent — these follicles are taken from the genetically stable donor area and will continue growing for life.
Is it safe to fly home after surgery?
Yes, flying is completely safe after hair transplant. Most patients board their return flight 3-5 days post-procedure. We provide a travel kit with medications, a loose-fitting hat, and medical documentation if needed for security. During the flight, stay hydrated, avoid pressing your scalp against the headrest, and take short walks periodically. Your surgeon will give you personalized travel guidance.
What if I don't speak Chinese?
No problem at all. From your first inquiry through final follow-up, you'll have a dedicated English-speaking coordinator. All medical documents, consent forms, and aftercare instructions are provided in English. Many of our surgeons speak fluent English. We also offer WhatsApp and WeChat support in English seven days a week. You'll never feel lost or unable to communicate.
How do I prepare for my trip to China?
We'll send you a detailed checklist after your consultation. Key steps: stop blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen) and certain supplements 1-2 weeks before surgery, avoid alcohol and smoking, arrange your visa (we provide an invitation letter), book flights, and pack loose-fitting clothes for after surgery. We recommend arriving in China at least one day before your procedure to settle in.
What happens after I return home?
We provide comprehensive remote follow-up care. You'll have video check-ins at 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, and 12 months. You can also reach your surgeon anytime via WhatsApp or email with questions. We send detailed recovery guides, photo tracking instructions, and milestone timelines so you know exactly what to expect. If any concerns arise, we're always available to help.
Can I combine my hair transplant with tourism?
Absolutely! Many patients turn their procedure into a memorable China experience. After the initial 5-7 day recovery, you can enjoy light sightseeing — visiting the Great Wall, exploring Shanghai's Bund, or experiencing local cuisine. We'll advise which activities are safe and how to protect your healing scalp from sun exposure. Just avoid strenuous exercise, swimming, or direct sun for the first two weeks.
What's the difference between FUE and FUT?
FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) removes individual follicles one by one from the donor area, leaving tiny dot scars that are virtually invisible. FUT (Follicular Unit Transplantation) removes a strip of skin, which is then dissected into individual follicles, leaving a linear scar. At Barley, we primarily use advanced microneedle FUE because it's less invasive, has faster recovery, and produces no visible scarring. FUT is reserved for special cases requiring maximum grafts in one session.
How do I know the before-and-after photos are real?
All photos on our website are actual patient results taken under standardized lighting with no digital alteration. During your consultation, we can show you cases similar to your specific hair loss pattern. We also encourage you to request verification — we can connect you with former international patients willing to share their honest experiences. Transparency is fundamental to how we operate.
What payment methods do you accept?
We accept Visa, MasterCard, bank wire transfers, cash (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.), and digital payments like Alipay and WeChat Pay. Payment is due on procedure day after you've reviewed the final treatment plan. For larger procedures, we offer flexible payment plans — just discuss this with your coordinator during consultation.
What if I need a touch-up or revision?
With our 95%+ graft survival rate, touch-ups are rare. However, if certain areas need refinement within the first 12 months, we provide complimentary minor adjustments. For international patients, we assess the situation via photos and video calls first. If a touch-up is warranted, we cover the surgical costs — you only arrange travel. Many patients combine their touch-up visit with another trip to China.
