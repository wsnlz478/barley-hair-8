Everything You Need to Know About Your Hair Transplant

Candid, clear-cut guidance from our medical professionals to empower your decision-making

What exactly happens during a hair transplant procedure?
A hair transplant involves moving healthy hair follicles from the permanent donor area at the back of your scalp to regions experiencing thinning or baldness. These donor follicles are genetically resistant to DHT—the hormone responsible for pattern baldness—so they continue thriving in their new location permanently. Modern techniques use individual follicular unit extraction combined with microneedle precision to deliver completely natural-looking results.
How do I know if I'm a good candidate for hair transplant?
Ideal candidates typically have stabilized hair loss, sufficient donor hair along the back and sides of the scalp, and realistic expectations about outcomes. Most surgeons recommend waiting until at least age 25, since hair loss patterns in your early twenties are often still changing. There's no strict upper age limit—people in their 60s and 70s achieve excellent results as long as they're in good health. Your surgeon will assess your candidacy during a free consultation.
How many grafts will I need for my procedure?
The graft count depends on how advanced your hair loss is and the area that needs coverage. A receding hairline (Norwood 2-3) typically requires 1,500-2,500 grafts. Moderate thinning (Norwood 4) usually needs 2,500-3,500 grafts. Extensive baldness (Norwood 5-7) may require 4,000-6,000 grafts, often split into two sessions. Your surgeon will give you an exact number after evaluating your donor supply and analyzing your scalp.
Is the procedure painful?
The entire procedure is performed under local anesthesia. The only notable sensation is a brief pinch from the initial anesthetic injections, lasting about 10-15 seconds. Once the scalp is numb, you'll feel gentle pressure but no pain. During the 6-8 hour surgery, you're free to watch movies, listen to music, eat, read, or even nap. Post-operative discomfort is generally mild and easily managed with prescribed pain medication.
How long does the surgery take?
A typical session involving 2,000-3,500 grafts usually takes 6-8 hours. Larger sessions exceeding 4,000 grafts may be split over two consecutive days to maintain optimal graft viability and patient comfort. The surgical team works in coordinated parallel, with extraction, preparation, and implantation happening simultaneously to minimize the time grafts spend outside the body.
When can I return to work after the procedure?
People with desk jobs can usually return within 2-3 days, often wearing a loose-fitting hat to conceal any visible redness. Those in physically demanding jobs should plan for 7-10 days off. We can provide a medical certificate for your employer upon request. Many international patients feel comfortable flying home within 5-7 days after the procedure.
What is shock loss and should I be worried about it?
Shock loss refers to the temporary shedding of transplanted hairs that typically occurs 2-6 weeks after surgery. Some native hairs near the recipient area may also shed briefly. This is a completely normal and expected part of the healing process. The follicles remain healthy beneath the skin and will start producing new visible hair within 3-4 months. Knowing this in advance prevents unnecessary anxiety.
When will I see visible results?
Hair growth follows a predictable timeline. Months 1-2: shedding phase when transplanted hairs fall out. Month 3: first fine new hairs begin emerging. Months 4-6: you'll notice real thickening and better coverage. Months 7-9: significant density gains become visible. Months 12-18: full maturation with maximum shaft thickness and final density. Patience is key—the best results develop gradually.
How much does a hair transplant cost in China?
At top clinics like Barley, prices typically range from $2,500 to $5,000 USD depending on the number of grafts and technique chosen. This represents savings of 50-70% compared to similar procedures in the United States, United Kingdom, or Australia, where equivalent treatment often costs $8,000-$15,000. Despite the lower cost, the technology, surgeon qualifications, and clinical standards match or exceed those of leading Western facilities.
What payment methods do you accept?
We accept international credit cards (Visa, Mastercard), bank wire transfers, Alipay, WeChat Pay, and cash in major currencies including CNY, USD, and EUR. A deposit secures your appointment date, with the remaining balance due on the day of your procedure. We provide official receipts and detailed invoices for your records.
Do you provide English-speaking services?
Absolutely. Our international patient department includes dedicated English-speaking coordinators and several surgeons who communicate fluently in English throughout the entire process. We also have staff fluent in Korean, Japanese, Arabic, Russian, and other languages. All consent forms, medical instructions, and aftercare guides are provided in English.
Can I have a video consultation before booking my flight?
Of course. We offer free remote consultations via WhatsApp, WeChat, Zoom, or email. Simply send clear photos of your scalp from the front, top, sides, and back. A qualified surgeon will assess your situation, estimate the graft count needed, recommend the most suitable technique, and provide a personalized treatment plan with transparent pricing. This allows you to make a confident decision before committing to travel.
Do you help with airport pickup and hotel booking?
Yes. We provide complimentary airport pickup from both Beijing Capital International Airport (PEK) and Daxing International Airport (PKX). We also assist with hotel bookings at partner properties near the clinic, typically at rates 15-20% lower than publicly listed prices. Your assigned coordinator handles all ground arrangements so you can focus entirely on recovery and enjoy your time in Beijing.
What visa do I need to travel to China for a hair transplant?
Most patients use a standard tourist (L) visa. Citizens of certain countries may qualify for 72-hour or 144-hour visa-free transit through designated Chinese airports, which often suffices for a hair transplant trip. For medical-specific entry, a short-term private affairs (S2) visa is also an option. We provide an official invitation letter from the clinic to support your visa application regardless of which category you choose. Visa requirements vary by nationality, so we recommend checking with your nearest Chinese embassy or consulate.
How are follow-up appointments handled after I return home?
Follow-up care is provided remotely through scheduled video calls and photo submissions at 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, and 12 months after surgery. Your operating surgeon personally reviews your progress at each milestone. We remain accessible via WhatsApp and email for any questions or concerns throughout your entire recovery journey. If you ever need an in-person check-up, we can coordinate with a trusted partner clinic in your region.
