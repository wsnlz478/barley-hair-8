What Causes Hair to Fall Out

Clinicians call it alopecia, but most people just know it as losing hair. If this is happening to you, understand that millions share the same struggle. The leading variety, androgenetic alopecia — often called male or female pattern baldness — originates from a hormone named DHT that progressively shrinks susceptible hair follicles. With every growth cycle, replacement strands grow thinner and shorter until production stops altogether. Although your genetic makeup dictates how vulnerable your follicles prove to be against DHT, the positive takeaway is that scientifically validated therapies can meaningfully decelerate, arrest, or sometimes turn around this decline regardless of how far it has advanced.

That said, inherited traits represent only part of the picture. Ongoing stress, poor dietary habits, thyroid imbalances, hormonal shifts, and immune system disorders can all speed up thinning or provoke sudden episodes of shedding. That is exactly why getting a precise diagnosis matters so much. At Barley Hospital, our experts perform thorough assessments — including blood work and high-resolution scalp scans — to pinpoint the precise origin of your hair loss before crafting a plan built around your individual needs.

Non-Surgical Approaches That Deliver Results

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) Therapy

In this treatment, a small amount of your blood is drawn, spun down to isolate the platelet concentration, and then the growth factor-packed plasma is injected straight into thinning zones. These platelets activate sluggish follicles, boost blood flow, and speed up cellular repair. A full course typically involves 3–6 appointments spaced a few weeks apart, and most people notice visible thickening within a matter of months.

Topical Minoxidil

This FDA-cleared topical is massaged into the scalp two times per day. It works by boosting circulation to the follicles and extending their growing phase. Sold over the counter in both 2% and 5% strengths, steady daily use is critical — most users see noticeable thickening around the 4–6 month mark. Appropriate for both male and female patients.

Oral Finasteride

This prescription drug blocks the enzyme that transforms testosterone into DHT — the very hormone behind hereditary balding. By cutting scalp DHT by roughly 70%, finasteride stops further loss and often triggers fresh growth, especially around the crown. Designed exclusively for men, it demands steady daily intake to keep the gains you have earned.

Low-Level Laser Therapy (LLLT)

LLLT uses targeted red light wavelengths (650–670nm) to energize follicle cells from within. The treatment ramps up ATP output, boosts micro-blood flow, and nudges follicles into their growing phase. Completely pain-free and non-invasive, sessions happen in our office or with FDA-cleared at-home units. It pairs remarkably well with other modalities for amplified results.

Why Layering Multiple Therapies Works Best

Unlock Better Outcomes with a Multi-Modal Plan

Years of clinical practice have confirmed one thing repeatedly: blending several treatments yields far superior outcomes compared to depending on just one method. Take an example — a transplant can fill in completely bare patches, yet drugs like finasteride and minoxidil shield the hair you still have. Adding PRP sessions after surgery quickens healing and lifts graft survival. At the same time, laser sessions create a healthier scalp foundation that magnifies every other treatment's impact. At Barley Hospital, we build bespoke protocols grounded in your particular loss pattern, how far it has progressed, and what you hope to achieve. Our records consistently show that patients who follow combined plans enjoy noticeably better outcomes than those sticking with a single approach.

Discovering the Right Approach for You

Choosing the best course of action depends on several key variables: what is driving your hair loss, how advanced it has become, your age, overall wellness, and personal preferences. In the beginning phases, medication by itself can yield impressive changes. Further-along cases usually call for surgical work paired with continuous medical management to hold onto what remains. The smartest first move is sitting down for a thorough professional consultation. At Barley Hospital, our doctors use advanced diagnostic tools — including trichoscopic imaging and in-depth scalp evaluation — to measure your situation with precision. We then walk you through every option with candid expectations, realistic timelines, and clear-cut pricing. No cookie-cutter answers here. We figure out what fits your circumstances best.

Common Questions About Hair Loss Treatment

Expert answers to guide your decisions

How many PRP sessions will I actually need before seeing results?
Most patients need an initial series of three to four PRP sessions spaced about four to six weeks apart before noticeable improvement shows up. After that initial round, maintenance sessions every six to twelve months help keep the gains. Some people see reduced shedding after just one or two treatments, but real density changes usually take the full series plus another two to three months for the new hairs to mature enough to see.
Is PRP therapy painful and how long does each session take?
Most patients describe PRP injections as a mild discomfort — we apply a topical numbing cream beforehand so the process is very manageable. The whole appointment, from blood draw to final injection, takes about 45 to 60 minutes. Some people feel light tenderness or tightness in the scalp for a day or two afterward, but there is no real downtime. You can go straight back to work or your daily routine.
Can I combine PRP with minoxidil and finasteride at the same time?
Absolutely — and we actually recommend it. Combining PRP with minoxidil and finasteride creates a multi-angle approach that works better than any single treatment alone. PRP delivers concentrated growth factors directly to the follicles, minoxidil boosts blood flow and extends the growth phase, and finasteride blocks the DHT that causes miniaturization in the first place. Patients on combination therapy consistently see better density and faster results than those using one method on its own.
What is the difference between minoxidil and finasteride, and do I need both?
They work in completely different ways. Minoxidil is a topical solution that increases blood flow to the follicles and prolongs their growth phase — it helps existing hairs grow thicker and wakes up sluggish ones. Finasteride is an oral tablet that blocks the conversion of testosterone to DHT, stopping the hormonal damage at the source. Whether you need one or both depends on your specific pattern and stage of loss. Many men benefit from using both together, while women typically use minoxidil alone or with other non-DHT-blocking options.
How long before I see visible improvement from non-surgical treatments?
It depends on which treatments you are using and how far along your hair loss is. Minoxidil users often notice reduced shedding within two to four weeks, with visible new growth around months three to six. PRP results typically become noticeable after two to three sessions, so roughly three to four months in. Finasteride may slow loss within three months, with regrowth showing around six to twelve months. The key is consistency — skipping applications or sessions will slow your progress.
Does low-level laser therapy actually work, or is it just marketing?
LLLT is backed by genuine clinical evidence. The red light wavelengths (650–670nm) penetrate the scalp and stimulate cellular energy production in the follicles — specifically boosting ATP output, which fuels hair growth. Multiple peer-reviewed studies have shown measurable improvements in hair density and thickness with regular use. It works especially well as a supporting treatment alongside PRP or medication. It is painless, non-invasive, and you can even use FDA-cleared devices at home between clinic visits.
Will I need to keep using these treatments forever?
For the most part, yes. Minoxidil and finasteride need to be continued to maintain the hair they have helped preserve or regrow. If you stop, the loss process will gradually resume. PRP therapy requires maintenance sessions every six to twelve months after the initial course. Think of it like exercise — you keep doing it to stay fit. During your consultation, we will discuss a realistic long-term maintenance plan that fits your lifestyle and budget.
Are there any side effects from PRP therapy since it uses my own blood?
Because PRP uses your own platelets, the risk of allergic reaction or rejection is essentially zero. The most common side effects are mild scalp tenderness, slight swelling, or minor bruising at the injection sites, all of which resolve within one to two days. Serious complications are extremely rare. Our doctors review your medical history before treatment to make sure PRP is appropriate for you.
I noticed more shedding after starting minoxidil — is that normal?
Yes, this is completely normal and actually a good sign. During the first two to eight weeks of minoxidil use, many people experience what is called a "dread shed." The medication pushes resting-phase hairs out to make room for new, healthier growth. This temporary increase in shedding typically subsides within a few weeks and is followed by visible regrowth. Do not stop the treatment during this phase — it means the medication is working.
Can non-surgical treatments fix a receding hairline, or do I need surgery?
It depends on how far the recession has gone. In early stages, a combination of minoxidil, finasteride (for men), and PRP can slow or partially reverse hairline recession by thickening miniaturized hairs along the temples. However, if the hairline has been receding for many years and the follicles are completely dormant, non-surgical treatments alone may not fully restore it. In that case, a hair transplant combined with ongoing medical therapy gives the best long-term result. A consultation helps determine where you stand.
How do you diagnose the exact cause of my hair loss?
We start with a detailed medical history covering your family background, stress levels, diet, medications, and any recent illnesses. Then we perform a digital trichoscopy — a magnified examination of your scalp that reveals follicle health, miniaturization patterns, and density measurements. We may also order blood work to check thyroid function, iron levels, vitamin D, and hormone panels. This comprehensive approach ensures we identify the true cause before recommending any treatment.
Is mesotherapy effective for hair regrowth?
Mesotherapy involves injecting a cocktail of vitamins, minerals, amino acids, and sometimes medications directly into the scalp to nourish follicles and improve circulation. Many patients see positive results, especially when combined with other treatments like PRP or minoxidil. The evidence base is not as strong as for PRP or FDA-approved medications, but it can be a useful supplementary therapy. Our doctors will advise whether mesotherapy makes sense for your specific situation during your consultation.
Can stress-related hair loss be treated without medication?
In many cases, yes. Telogen effluvium — the type of hair loss triggered by stress — often resolves on its own once the stressor is removed. Supporting your recovery with good nutrition, adequate sleep, scalp massage, and gentle care can speed things up. PRP therapy and low-level laser can also accelerate regrowth without any pharmaceutical intervention. However, if the stress has unmasked an underlying genetic tendency, medication may be needed to prevent ongoing loss. We will help you figure out which scenario applies to you.
How much do non-surgical hair loss treatments cost at Barley?
Costs vary depending on the treatment plan. A full PRP course of three to four sessions typically runs £800–£1,500, compared to £2,000–£4,000 in the UK or US. Monthly minoxidil costs around £10–£20, and finasteride is similarly affordable. LLLT devices for home use range from £200–£500. During your free consultation, we provide a complete cost breakdown with no hidden fees so you know exactly what to expect.
Can women use the same treatments as men for hair loss?
Some treatments overlap, but women's hair loss often has different causes and requires a tailored approach. Minoxidil is FDA-approved for women (usually 2% twice daily or 5% once daily). PRP therapy works well for both genders. However, finasteride is generally not prescribed for women of childbearing age due to birth-defect risks. For women, we may recommend spironolactone instead, along with nutritional support and hormone balancing. Our doctors design treatment plans specifically for each patient's unique situation.
Do you offer remote consultations for international patients?
Yes. We offer free virtual consultations where you send us clear photos of your scalp from multiple angles, along with your medical history. Our doctors review everything and provide a detailed assessment and treatment recommendation. For treatments like PRP that require in-person visits, we help you plan your trip around the treatment schedule and arrange follow-up care that continues via video calls once you return home. Our English-speaking coordinators handle everything from start to finish.
